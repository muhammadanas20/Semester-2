## 🎓 KEY FINDINGS

### #1: CLO 5 is The Main Focus ⭐⭐⭐
- **40-60% of total exam marks**
- Includes: Counters, Shift Registers, Sequential Circuits
- Most complex problems appear here
- Requires full design methodology knowledge

### #2: Custom Sequence Counters Are Critical ⭐⭐
- Present in 100% of advanced exams
- 15-25 marks per exam
- Requires: State table, K-Maps, circuit, timing diagram
- Most time-consuming question

### #3: K-Map Mastery is Non-Negotiable ⭐⭐
- Every exam has K-Map problems
- 10-15 marks guaranteed
- 4-variable K-Maps most common
- Both SOP and POS implementations tested

### #4: Exam Structure is Consistent
- Question 1: Always number systems (5 marks)
- Question 2: Always Boolean/K-Map (10 marks)
- Question 3: Always combinational circuits (10 marks)
- Question 4-6: Sequential circuits, counters, shift registers (25 marks)

### #5: Personalization Elements
- 2020 exam: Heavily personalized (roll number dependent)
- 2025 exam: More structured with fixed sequences
- 2023 exam: Complex multi-part state machines
